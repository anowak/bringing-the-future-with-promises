The rationale

  • Applications in JavaScript are highly asynchronous
  • Relaying only on callbacks quickly lead to Callback hell
  • Error handling tends to violate DRY and is hard to trace
  • There is a strong need for a model that is easy to reason about
12

Promises 101

  • A promise represents the eventual value returned from the single completion of an operation.
  • A promise may be in one of the three states, unfulfilled, fulfilled, and failed
  • Has then(fulfilledHandler, errorHandler, progressHandler) function
    • 20

    Common.js Promises/A

  • One of the proposals for the promises specification
  • Defines promise as the object with properties from the previous slide (and few others, like progress handling)
  • The most widespread in terms of implementation
  • Does not specify how promises are constructed
  • Easy to reason about because of similarity to try/catch blocks
    • 23

    Promises/A+

  • Removes some details e.g. about interactive promises and progress notifications
  • Does not specify how promises are constructed
  • Adds semantics for returning promises in the callbacks
